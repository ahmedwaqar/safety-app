## Ninety-Second Compression

For each story, write:

1. **Opening, 15 seconds:** system, role, and problem.
2. **Method, 30 seconds:** constraints, analysis, and people involved.
3. **Decision, 25 seconds:** recommendation, alternative, and evidence.
4. **Outcome, 10 seconds:** result and residual risk.
5. **Learning, 10 seconds:** changed practice.

## Follow-Up Defense

Prepare concise answers to:

1. What was your personal contribution?
2. What evidence did you rely on?
3. What assumption was most uncertain?
4. What alternative did you reject?
5. Who approved the decision?
6. What would have changed your recommendation?
7. What did you get wrong initially?
8. How would the decision change at a higher integrity or consequence level?

## Honesty Check

Before rehearsing, label each story:

- `Professional`: work you actually performed
- `Observed`: work you witnessed but did not own
- `Simulation`: work completed through this training

State the label honestly during the interview when it affects how the question is answered.
